By the early 2000's years, the internet had finally become a household name. As computer technology rapidly improved and became cheaper, it no longer was just a tool for researchers and academics, but a platform for creativity, communication, and commerce. Personal websites, blogs and forums became widely popular as the adoption of the internet across the globe continued.

For those familiar with new internet terms, the design style of this era would largely fall into the 'webcore' definition. The distinct characteristics of this style include the use of bright colours (as most sites were made by ameatures to design) and flashy gifs such as what you see now.

Adobe flash player

It is hard to understate the impact that Adobe Flash Player had on web design. It was a platform that allowed for the creation of interactive content, animations, and games, which were not possible with HTML alone. This combined with the popularisation of personal blogs meant that artists and other creatives were picking up web design as a real creative outlet. Although it released in 1996, it was not until the early 2000's that it became more adopted by other creatives.

Games on the web also started becoming more popular with Adobe Flash Player. You might recodnise the early popularisation of sites such as Newgrounds, then later Kongregate and Armorgames. These sites would host games made in flash, and would allow users to play them for free. This was a major shift in the way that people interacted with the web, as it allowed for immersive worlds and experiences to be readily available for anyone with a web browser to play.
